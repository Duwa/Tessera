Federation model

The corporation of the 2030s
is a network, not a monolith

Large organizations are already becoming federations of smaller, AI-optimized units under a shared brand. HACM is the operating layer that makes that work — each unit autonomous, the whole coherent.

Shared beliefs
Every unit in the federation has its own belief template. The brand core holds the values that bind them. HACM shows where they align and where they're drifting.
Independent economics
Each unit runs its own token budget, payroll, and cost-benefit analysis. The federation sees aggregate AI ROI across all units — without losing the unit-level signal.
Capital mobility
People and AI agents move between units. HACM tracks belief fit across the transition — so the unit receiving a new capital unit knows what they're getting.

Platform · Step-by-step guide

How to use the
Platform module.

The Platform page has three live tools — the Digital Twin, the Cost-Benefit analyser, and the Agent Fleet monitor. This guide walks through each one step by step.

3 tools ~5 min read
Tool 1 of 3
Digital Twin

A live simulation of your organisation — every person and every AI agent rendered as a belief-space position, updated in real time as they learn, decide, and adapt.

01
Run the simulation
Click the green ▶ Run simulation button in the hero bar or inside the twin panel. The simulation advances in ticks — each tick is one round of decisions across the org.
💡 The K selector in the top bar controls landscape ruggedness. K=2 is smooth and easy to optimise. K=6 is complex — only hybrid human + AI orgs thrive.
02
Read the belief space canvas
The central canvas shows your org as a scatter of dots. Green dots are people, blue dots are AI agents. Dot size = fitness. Glowing dots are high performers. The faint square is the org belief template — the shared target all units orient toward.
💡 Dots drifting toward the template = belief alignment improving. Scattered dots = high observer-dependence or active adaptation.
03
Interpret the Org Health panel
Four KPIs update live on the right panel:

Fitness — overall org performance on the NK landscape (0–1).
Belief align — how tightly the org clusters around its template.
Decisions — total decisions made this run.
Rebalances — how many times the system re-allocated human/AI roles.
04
Track fitness over time
The bottom-left panel shows a fitness trajectory chart — each point is one consolidation epoch (every 50 ticks). A healthy org shows an upward curve that flattens as it reaches the fitness peak for its landscape.
💡 Flat or declining trajectory at K=6 means you need more AI agents or a change in org belief template.
05
Observer dependence (SNR)
The bottom-right panel shows the same org from four different reference frames. Different observers measure different fitness values from identical data — this is observer-dependence. The SNR number shows how much signal vs noise different observers see. Research baseline: 3.3–4.1×.
06
Live decisions log
The scrolling log beneath the Org Health panel shows every decision as it fires: agent name, decision type (Resolved, Oversight, Flight risk, Token limit), and the capital unit it came from. This is your real-time audit trail.
💡 High "Flight" counts mean agents are hitting belief-misalignment thresholds — time to rebalance the roster.
Tool 2 of 3
Cost-Benefit Analyser

Every organisation has a minimum token investment below which AI participation generates negative net value. HACM finds your B* threshold and shows exactly where you sit on the frontier curve.

01
Set your organisation size
Use the first two sliders to set Number of people (10–500) and AI agents deployed (0–100). These define your hybrid workforce ratio — the fundamental input to the Huang model.
💡 A good starting point: your real headcount and however many AI tools your team actively uses daily.
02
Set token budget and salary
The Monthly AI token budget slider sets your total spend on AI inference (API costs, LLM subscriptions). The Avg salary slider feeds the Huang benchmark — the research-grounded target of 50% of base salary in annual token allocation per knowledge worker.
03
Read your position on the frontier
The frontier chart plots net org fitness vs monthly token investment. Your org appears as a marked point. Five regimes:

Below B* — spending money, getting nothing back.
Near B* — marginal territory, invest more or cut.
Baseline / Abundance — positive returns.
Saturation — diminishing returns, optimise allocation instead.
04
Understand the KPI panel
Four numbers update as you move sliders:

AI return — ROI on your token spend.
Huang ratio — your spend vs the 50%-of-salary benchmark.
Your B* — your minimum viable monthly token spend.
Huang target — what Huang model says you should be spending.
05
Token allocation by role
The second chart shows how your budget distributes across role types (executive, senior IC, junior IC, operations, AI-native). Each bar reflects the Huang model's allocation — heavier cognitive roles draw more tokens. Use this to spot roles that are under-resourced.
💡 If your AI-native workers have less than 60% of their Huang target, your agents are compute-starved.
06
Act on the advice panel
Below the KPIs, the advice card gives a plain-language recommendation based on your position: whether to increase spend to cross B*, maintain current level, or start optimising allocation across roles. It updates in real time with every slider move.
💡 Use Change Impact (separate module) before acting — it shows how adding agents affects org fitness trajectory.
Tool 3 of 3
Agent Fleet & Organic IT

AI agents embedded in your infrastructure act as the IT layer. They continuously scan for vulnerabilities, surface signals as Cases, and route them automatically — with no separate IT ops team required.

01
Read the fleet KPI bar
Six numbers at the top of the Fleet section give an instant health summary: Active agents, Healthy, Warning, Critical, Cases auto-raised, and Infra zones covered. Green = all clear. Any red means a case has already been auto-raised.
02
Interpret agent status dots
Each agent card on the left has a coloured dot:

● Green (solid glow) — healthy, clean last scan.
● Amber — warning, vulnerabilities detected, monitoring.
● Red (pulsing) — critical, active threat signal, case already raised.
💡 A pulsing red dot means the agent has already auto-created a Case in Case Management — go there to see routing and status.
03
Read each agent card
Every card shows: agent name and the infrastructure it monitors, its role (what it's scanning for), coverage tags (specific services watched), the time of its last scan, and a vulnerability count or "Clean" status. Hover a card to highlight it.
04
Triage signals in the feed
The right column shows the live signal feed. Each entry has a severity dot:

● High — active threat (e.g. C2 beacon, exfil attempt).
● Medium — policy violation or resource exhaustion.
● Low — stale credentials, config drift.

Every signal shows the Case ID it was routed to — click to go to Case Management.
05
Check infrastructure coverage
The six coverage tiles in the bottom-right show which infra zones are being watched: Cloud, Identity, Endpoints, SaaS, Network, and Data. Green border = fully covered and clean. No border = signals raised in this zone. Any uncovered zone is a blind spot — add an agent.
06
Refresh scan
The ↻ Refresh scan button in the top-right of the Fleet section triggers all agents to re-poll their infrastructure simultaneously. Scan timestamps update on each card. In a live deployment, agents poll continuously — the refresh button gives you an on-demand sweep.
💡 In production, agents self-refresh on a configurable cadence (default: 60s). The Refresh button is for manual sweeps before a planned change.
